Lung Cancer: Comprehensive Guide

📋 Overview

Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths worldwide. It begins in the lungs and can spread to other parts of the body. There are two main types: non-small cell lung cancer (most common) and small cell lung cancer.

🚩 Common Symptoms

Persistent cough that worsens
Coughing up blood
Shortness of breath
Chest pain
Hoarseness
Unexplained weight loss

⚠️ When to See a Doctor Immediately

If you have a persistent cough for more than 3 weeks, cough up blood, or experience unexplained chest pain, seek medical attention immediately.

⚠️ Risk Factors

Smoking

Responsible for 85% of cases

Radon Gas

Second leading cause

Asbestos

Occupational exposure

Family History

Genetic factors

🛡️ Prevention

  • Don't smoke or quit smoking
  • Avoid secondhand smoke
  • Test your home for radon
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly

📋 Overview

Brain cancer occurs when abnormal cells form in brain tissue. Tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Primary brain tumors start in the brain, while metastatic tumors spread from other parts of the body.

🚩 Common Symptoms

Frequent headaches that worsen
Seizures
Nausea and vomiting
Vision problems
Balance difficulties
Personality changes

🚨 Emergency Symptoms

Sudden severe headache, seizures, vision loss, or difficulty speaking require immediate medical attention.

⚠️ Risk Factors

Family History

Genetic syndromes

Radiation

Previous exposure

Age

Risk increases with age

Weakened Immunity

Immune system disorders

📋 Overview

Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women worldwide. It forms in breast cells and can spread to other parts of the body. Early detection through screening significantly improves survival rates.

🚩 Common Symptoms

New lump in breast or underarm
Thickening or swelling
Skin irritation or dimpling
Nipple discharge or retraction
Breast pain
Redness or scaliness

🎗️ Self-Examination Guide

Perform monthly self-exams: in shower, in front of mirror, and lying down. Report any changes to your doctor immediately.

⚠️ Risk Factors

Being Female

Primary risk factor

Age >50

Risk increases with age

BRCA Genes

Genetic mutations

Family History

First-degree relatives

🛡️ Prevention & Screening

  • Monthly self-exams starting at age 20
  • Clinical breast exams every 1-3 years
  • Mammograms starting at age 40-45
  • Maintain healthy weight and exercise
  • Limit alcohol consumption
